# Website Copy & Blog Posts

## Modern Web Copywriting (2025-2026)

The shift: Away from "keyword stuff to rank" toward "write for humans first, optimize second." 80% of consumers now resolve 40% of searches without clicking (zero-click searches). Winners write copy that converts, not just ranks.

## Headlines That Convert

> **This is the most important section.** 80% of readers never get past the headline. For the full research, formulas, and testing methodology, see [headlines.md](headlines.md).

**Formula**: Numbers + Adjective + Target Keyword + Rationale + Promise

Examples:
- "5 Proven Tactics Professional Salespeople Use to Close 40% More Deals"
- "The 14-Day Email Sequence That Generated $150K in Revenue"

Research-backed headline statistics:
- Numbers in headlines: +30% conversion
- 8-word headlines: +21% conversion
- Action-oriented copy: +93% conversion
- Benefit-driven vs feature-driven: +85% conversion
- Specific outcomes: +321% conversion

Test 20 alternatives per headline (Ogilvy's rule). The first idea is rarely the best.

## Content Structure for Scanning

Users read only 20-28% of blog text. Structure for skimming:

- **Single H1** per page (main title, include primary keyword)
- **H2s** divide into clear sections (readers jump to what they need)
- **H3s** break down H2 sections into specifics
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- Bullet points for key information
- White space is critical for visual hierarchy

Articles with H2 and H3 subheadings see **36% higher performance** in traffic, shares, and backlinks.

## SEO Without Sacrificing Readability

- Write first (for humans), optimize second (for search)
- Focus on EEAT: Expertise, Experience, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness
- Keywords fit naturally, never forced
- Answer the search intent fully (what is the reader actually looking for?)
- Include specific, actionable information
- AI search optimization matters now: structure content so AI can extract clear answers

## CTAs That Convert

**Requirements:**
- Visual prominence (stands out on page)
- Specific clarity (tell them exactly what happens after click)
- Action verbs: Get, Start, Claim, Learn, Download, Discover

**Impact data:**
- Single well-placed CTA: +371% click-through
- Personalized CTAs: +42% over standard
- Behavior-based dynamic CTAs: +44% boost

**Examples:**
- "Get instant access to our free report"
- "Start your free 7-day trial today"
- "Claim your 20% discount before midnight"

## Service Page Structure

For service/practice area pages:

1. **H1**: [Service] + [Location] (e.g., "Estate Planning Attorney in The Woodlands, TX")
2. **Opening**: What the reader is dealing with + how you solve it
3. **Process section**: Step-by-step what working with you looks like (transparency wins)
4. **Types/Options**: Compare approaches so reader can self-qualify
5. **Cost transparency**: Address pricing honestly (competitors avoid this, readers want it)
6. **Social proof**: Reviews, case studies, credentials
7. **FAQ**: Address objections as questions
8. **CTA**: Clear next step with specific outcome

## Blog Post Template

```
# [Headline with specific number/outcome]

[Hook paragraph: surprising fact, question, or bold claim]

## [Section 1: Problem/Context]
[2-3 paragraphs with specific evidence]

## [Section 2: Analysis/Solution]
[2-3 paragraphs, bullet points for key items]

## [Section 3: Implications/Action Steps]
[What should the reader do with this information?]

## Key Takeaways
- [Specific, actionable point 1]
- [Specific, actionable point 2]
- [Specific, actionable point 3]

[CTA: clear next step]
```

## Answer-First Article Structure (for AEO)

When the page is meant to rank in AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews), use this structure instead of the standard blog template. AI models extract answers from the first 30% of content 44% of the time, so the lead paragraph is the page's most valuable real estate.

For the full audit framework and citability scoring, see the seo plugin's AEO reference: `plugins/seo/skills/seo/reference/aeo.md`. The rules below are the writing-side distillation.

**Lead with the answer:**
- First paragraph: **30-60 word neutral, factual summary** that directly answers the page's primary question. Write it the way you'd want ChatGPT to quote it back. This is the paragraph AI will lift verbatim.
- No hook, no anecdote, no "in this article we'll cover" — those come after the answer block.
- Use definitive language: "X is the best for Y because Z" beats "X might be good for Y." AI prefers confident, verifiable assertions.

**Question-formatted H2s:**
- Phrase every major heading as a real user question. Question-style H2s yield +17.54% similarity lift for AI citation and are 84% more likely to trigger AI Overviews.
- Examples: "What is X?" / "How does X work?" / "When should you use X?" / "What does X cost?" / "How is X different from Y?"

**Self-contained answer blocks between headings:**
- Each section is a **120-180 word answer block** that stands on its own. No pronoun references to previous sections ("it", "this", "that" referring back).
- Atomic paragraphs: 1-3 sentences each, dense and quotable.
- Lead each block with the answer; supporting detail follows.

**Required structural assets:**
- **Comparison table** for any "X vs Y" or feature/option comparison (2.5-2.8x citation rate vs prose).
- **Numbered or bulleted list** for any process, ranking, or set of items (listicles account for 50% of top AI citations).
- **FAQ section** at the end with 5+ Q&As pulled from real user queries (72% citation rate vs 34% for prose-only).
- **Statistics with attribution** sprinkled throughout: percentages, dollar amounts, dated numbers, named sources ("according to Semrush 2026...").

**Answer-first template:**

```
# [Question or definitive claim as headline]

[30-60 word direct answer. Neutral, factual, verbatim-quotable. Define the term, state the answer, name the entity.]

## What is [X]?
[120-180 word self-contained answer block. Definition + scope + who it applies to.]

## How does [X] work?
[120-180 word block. Mechanism, ordered steps, named components.]

## [X] vs [Y]: which is right for you?
[Comparison table — 4-7 rows, 3-5 columns. Lead with the dimensions buyers actually decide on.]

## When does [X] fail?
[120-180 word block. Honest failure modes; AI rewards balanced coverage.]

## How much does [X] cost?
[Specific numbers, ranges, what drives variance. Transparency wins.]

## Frequently asked questions
**[Real question 1 from user research]**
[60-100 word answer.]

**[Real question 2]**
[60-100 word answer.]

[5+ FAQs total]

[CTA: clear next step]
```

**When to use answer-first vs the standard template:**

| Use answer-first when... | Use the standard blog template when... |
|--------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Page targets a high-intent informational query ("what is", "how to", "X vs Y", "best X for Y") | Piece is opinion, narrative, or analysis where the hook matters more than extractability |
| Goal is AI citation / AEO ranking | Goal is engagement, shares, or brand voice |
| Page is a service description, comparison, FAQ, or definitional guide | Piece is a personal essay, case study narrative, or thought leadership |
| Search intent is "give me the answer" | Search intent is "tell me a story" or "convince me" |

## Email Newsletter Copy

- Subject lines: adversarial quality (test multiple, pick the strongest)
- Intro summary: 3-5 sentences, standalone value without reading full content
- Source attribution: cite where information comes from
- Standardized CTA format per brand
- No "today's" references if content may be reused
